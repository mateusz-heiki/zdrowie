Magnesium Forms — Bioavailability Comparison

Not all magnesium is equal. The form determines how much your body actually absorbs. This product uses bisglycinate chelate — one of the most bioavailable forms, bound to the amino acid glycine for gentle absorption without GI distress.

Magnesium Bisglycinate
Mg(C₂H₄NO₂)₂
Bioavailability~80%
Elemental Mg14.1%
GI ToleranceExcellent
Typical Dose200–400 mg

Chelated to glycine via TRAACS® process. Absorbed through amino acid pathways, bypassing common mineral transport bottlenecks. Best for sleep, stress, and muscle recovery. Used in this product.

Magnesium Citrate
Mg₃(C₆H₅O₇)₂
Bioavailability~65%
Elemental Mg16.2%
GI ToleranceModerate
Typical Dose200–400 mg

Bound to citric acid. Good absorption but may cause loose stools at higher doses. Popular general-purpose form, widely used in clinical studies.

Magnesium L-Threonate
Mg(C₄H₇O₅)₂
Bioavailability~70%
Elemental Mg7.2%
GI ToleranceGood
Typical Dose1,500–2,000 mg

Patented Magtein® form. Uniquely crosses the blood-brain barrier. Best for cognitive function and memory. Very low elemental Mg — not ideal as sole magnesium source.

Magnesium Taurate
Mg(C₂H₆NO₃S)₂
Bioavailability~60%
Elemental Mg8.9%
GI ToleranceExcellent
Typical Dose200–400 mg

Bound to taurine amino acid. Favored for cardiovascular support due to synergistic taurine effects on heart rhythm and blood pressure regulation.

Magnesium Malate
MgC₄H₄O₅
Bioavailability~55%
Elemental Mg15.1%
GI ToleranceGood
Typical Dose200–400 mg

Bound to malic acid (from apples). Popular for energy and fibromyalgia support. Malic acid is involved in the Krebs cycle, making it relevant for ATP production.

Magnesium Oxide
MgO
Bioavailability~4%
Elemental Mg60.3%
GI TolerancePoor
Typical Dose400–800 mg

Cheapest form with highest elemental Mg but extremely poor absorption (~4%). Primarily acts as an osmotic laxative. Not recommended for correcting deficiency.

Cofactors in This Formula
Vitamin B6 (P5P)
Active coenzyme form of B6. Facilitates magnesium transport into cells via membrane channels. Studies show 24% higher intracellular Mg retention when co-administered with P5P vs. magnesium alone.
Taurine
Conditionally essential amino acid that synergizes with magnesium for cardiac and neural function. Supports GABA receptor sensitivity, amplifying Mg's calming effects on the nervous system.
Zinc Bisglycinate
Low-dose chelated zinc (5 mg) supports over 300 enzymatic reactions that also require magnesium as a cofactor. Chelated form avoids GI irritation and competitive absorption issues.
